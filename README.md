# HR Analytics: Employee Retention & Attrition Strategy  
**Workforce Dynamics Analysis | Data-Driven HR Decision Support**

---

## 📌 Project Overview  
This project delivers a comprehensive **HR Analytics framework** focused on understanding workforce dynamics, identifying the root causes of employee attrition, and optimizing retention strategies.

By leveraging **Power BI for advanced visualization** and **Python for data engineering**, the project transforms raw HR data into **actionable insights** that support strategic decision-making, reduce turnover costs, and improve organizational stability.

---

## 📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- **Total Workforce:** 1,470 employees  
- **Average Tenure:** 7 years  
- **Attrition Rate:** 16% (Primary retention benchmark)  
- **Average Monthly Income:** IDR 102.75M  

📌 Insight: Competitive compensation shows a **direct correlation with lower attrition**, but is not the sole driver of retention.

---

## 🔍 Strategic Insights (Root Cause Analysis)

### ⏱️ 1. Overtime as a Primary Risk Driver
Employees with frequent **overtime (OT)** exhibit significantly higher attrition rates.  
This indicates potential **burnout risk**, especially in high-demand departments.

---

### 🚗 2. Commute Fatigue & Work-Life Balance
A strong correlation exists between **distance from home** and resignation rates.  
Employees with longer commutes tend to have higher attrition, highlighting the importance of **work-life balance**.

---

### 📉 3. The "One-Year" Critical Attrition Point
Attrition spikes significantly within the **first year of employment**.  
This suggests gaps in:
- Onboarding effectiveness  
- Early employee engagement  
- Role expectation alignment  

---

### 🏆 4. Performance vs Retention Gap
Despite higher salary increases for **top performers**, attrition remains high in:
- Research & Development  
- Sales  

📌 Key Insight:  
Retention is influenced more by **career growth and job involvement** than compensation alone.

---

## 💡 Data-Driven Recommendations

### 1. Overtime Control Policy
- Implement OT monitoring systems  
- Limit excessive workloads in high-risk departments  
- Introduce workload balancing strategies  

---

### 2. Flexible Work Strategy
- Offer **hybrid / remote work options** for employees with long commutes  
- Improve work-life balance to reduce resignation probability  

---

### 3. Year-One Retention Program
- Structured onboarding framework  
- Mentorship programs for new hires  
- Early engagement tracking within first 12 months  

---

### 4. Promotion & Career Path Optimization
- Establish clear promotion pathways  
- Address stagnation for employees in the same role >3 years  
- Improve transparency in career progression
